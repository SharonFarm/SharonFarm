Plant-Based Products

Banana

Scientific Name: Musa acuminata / Musa balbisiana

Lifespan/Growing Period:
  • 9 to 12 months from planting to harvest
  • Perennial herb, each stalk fruits only once
Environmental Benefits:
  • Prevents soil erosion with large leaves
  • Organic waste enriches soil through composting
Health Benefits:
  • High in potassium for heart health
  • Rich in fiber, vitamin C, and B6

Mulberry

Scientific Name: Morus alba L.

Lifespan/Growing Period:
  • Mulberry trees live 25 to 100 years. Full maturity is reached in 10 to 15 years, with early fruiting possible at 3 years.
Environmental Benefits:
  • Soil Stabilization: Mulberry trees prevent soil erosion with their strong root systems.
  • Biodiversity: Provide food and habitat for birds and beneficial insects, enhancing biodiversity in urban gardens.
Health Benefits:
  • High in Vitamin C and iron.
  • Rich in antioxidants, particularly resveratrol, supporting immune function and improving blood circulation.
  • Rich in phenolic acids, which may help with cancer prevention and diabetes management..

Talong

Scientific Name: Solanum melongena

Lifespan/Growing Period:
  • One year (requires a long, warm growing season), The growing season lasts 2-3 months
Environmental Benefits:
  • Reduced Pesticide Use: Genetically modified eggplant varieties, resistant to fruit and shoot borers, reduce the need for pesticide applications compared to conventional eggplant.
Health Benefits:
  • Rich in Vitamin A and Vitamin C, supporting immune function and skin health.
  • High in fiber and antioxidants.
  • A serving provides at least 5% of the daily recommended intake of fiber, copper,manganese, B-6, and thiamine.

Luya

Scientific Name: Zingiber officinale

Lifespan/Growing Period:
  • Perennial plant (lives for more than two years). Ginger reaches full maturity in 210-240 days after planting, with harvesting for vegetable purposes starting around 180 days, depending on demand.
Environmental Benefits:
  • Thrives in hot, humid conditions and nutrient-rich soil.
  • Known for anti-inflammatory and anti-nausea properties, offering numerous health benefits.
Health Benefits:
  • Contains antioxidants that help manage free radicals, reducing cell damage.
  • May reduce the risk of diabetes, cancer, and other health problems.
  • Supports weight loss, arthritis management, and reduces menstrual symptoms.

Calamansi

Scientific Name: Citrus microcarpa

Lifespan/Growing Period:
  • Perennial plant. Calamansi trees begin bearing fruit in about 1-2 years, and fruit can be harvested year-round once mature.
Environmental Benefits:
  • Thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
  • Calamansi trees can help improve soil fertility and reduce erosion through their root systems.
Health Benefits:
  • Rich in Vitamin C, which supports immune health and skin health.
  • Contains antioxidants that may reduce inflammation and help fight infections.

Siling Labuyo

Scientific Name: Capsicum frutescens

Lifespan/Growing Period:
  • Perennial plant. Perennial in tropical climates, often grown as an annual, Grows well in sunny areas with moist, well-drained soil.
Environmental Benefits:
  • Siling Labuyo naturally repels pests like aphids and mites, making it a valuable companion plant in gardens.
  • It also attracts beneficial insects such as pollinators and ladybugs, supporting ecosystem health and improving crop yields.
Health Benefits:
  • High in Vitamin C, boosting the immune system and promoting healthy skin.
  • Contains Capsaicin, which has been shown to boost metabolism, reduce pain, and support weight loss.
  • Known for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, it may help manage conditions like arthritis.
  • Traditionally used in medicine to relieve pain, improve digestion, and reduce congestion.

Tanglad

Scientific Name: Cymbopogon citratus

Lifespan/Growing Period:
  • One year, though it can live longer in tropical climates, Requires a warm, sunny environment with well-drained soil.
Environmental Benefits:
  • Lemongrass naturally repels pests like mosquitoes due to its high citronella content, reducing the need for chemical pesticides and promoting organic farming practices.
Health Benefits:
  • Rich in Vitamin A and Vitamin C, promoting skin health and immune function.
  • Aids in digestion and supports detoxification.
  • Known for its antibacterial and antifungal properties, helping to fight infections.

Okra

Scientific Name: Cymbopogon citratus

Lifespan/Growing Period:
  • 120 days (approximately 4 months).
  • Early harvest can be done about one month earlier, and late harvest about one month later, optimizing the okra plant lifespan for commercial fiber extraction.
Environmental Benefits:
  • Okra is a biodegradable and eco-friendly alternative to synthetic polymers, making it a suitable choice for sustainable applications in industries such as packaging, textiles, and more.
Health Benefits:
  • High in Vitamin C, boosting immune function and skin health.
  • Rich in Vitamin K, which supports bone health and helps with blood clotting.

Upo

Scientific Name: Lagenaria siceraria

Lifespan/Growing Period:
  • Typically matures in 2 to 3 months after planting
  • Grown as an annual vine, it produces round, hard-shelled fruits often harvested for food or crafts.
Environmental Benefits:
  • The plant can be used in permaculture systems, with vines that provide natural shade and ground cover
  • The hard shells are biodegradable and can replace plastic in utensils, containers, or décor
Health Benefits:
  • Low in calories and rich in vitamin C, fiber, and antioxidants
  • Known for its cooling properties and use in traditional medicine to support digestion and hydration.

Alugbati

Scientific Name: Basella alba

Lifespan/Growing Period:
  • Perennial, but often grown as an annual in home gardens.
  • Thrives in hot, humid climates and grows quickly during warm seasons.
Environmental Benefits:
  • Alugbati forms a dense ground cover, helping to prevent erosion and retain soil moisture.
  • Pruned leaves enrich the soil with organic matter, supporting soil health.
Health Benefits:
  • Rich in vitamin C, fiber, and antioxidants
  • Known for cooling effects and supports digestion and hydration in traditional medicine

Malunggay

Scientific Name: Moringa oleifera

Lifespan/Growing Period:
  • Perennial, can live 30 to 50 years. Thrives in tropical to subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.
Environmental Benefits:
  • Malunggay is drought-resistant and grows in poor soils, making it ideal for reforestation and climate-resilient farming.
Health Benefits:
  • Rich in Vitamins A, B, C, calcium, iron, and amino acids.
  • Supports immune health, digestion, and promotes healthy skin and eyes.
  • Leaves, pods, and seeds are edible and used in traditional medicine.

Caimito

Scientific Name: Chrysophyllum cainito

Lifespan/Growing Period:
  • Perennial, can live for several decades. Grows best in warm climates with fertile, well-drained soil.
Environmental Benefits:
  • Caimito trees absorb CO₂ and offer habitat for local wildlife, improving air quality and ecosystem health.
Health Benefits:
  • Fruits are rich in Vitamin C, antioxidants, and fiber
  • Known for their sweet, juicy flesh and star-shaped cross-section
  • Leaves and bark are used in traditional medicine.

Papaya

Scientific Name: Carica papaya

Lifespan/Growing Period:
  • Short-lived perennial, productive for 3 to 5 years. Grows best in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.
Environmental Benefits:
  • Fast-growing trees that control weeds, provide shade, and improve soil fertility when composted.
Health Benefits:
  • Rich in Vitamin C, Vitamin A, and papain enzyme.
  • Aids digestion, boosts immunity, and promotes skin health.
  • Almost all parts are edible - fruit, seeds, and leaves.

Mangga

Scientific Name: Mangifera indica

Lifespan/Growing Period:
  • Long-lived perennial, can live for 100+ years. Thrives in tropical climates with full sun and well-drained soil.
Environmental Benefits:
  • Carbon Absorption & Biodiversity Support: Large trees absorb CO₂ and offer shelter for birds and pollinators, enhancing local biodiversity.
Health Benefits:
  • High in Vitamin A, Vitamin C, and antioxidants.
  • Supports immune health, eye health, and digestion.
  • Used in juices, salads, desserts, and preserves.

Madre de Agua

Scientific Name: Trichanthera gigantea

Lifespan/Growing Period:
  • Perennial. Thrives in tropical climates, prefers moist, well-drained soil and sun to partial shade.
Environmental Benefits:
  • Fast-growing shrub used as forage for livestock; stabilizes soil and prevents erosion, aiding in sustainable farming.
Health Benefits:
  • High in protein and nutrients, reduces feed costs.
  • Leaves used in green manure, composting, and traditional medicine

lorem

Pandan

Scientific Name: Pandanus amaryllifolius

Lifespan/Growing Period:
  • Perennial, can live for many years. Grows well in tropical climates, prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
Environmental Benefits:
  • Pandan grows densely, helping prevent soil erosion. It's low maintenance, making it ideal for sustainable urban gardens.
Health Benefits:
  • Leaves are widely used as a natural flavoring and aromatic in cooking.
  • Contains antioxidants and may have anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties.
  • Used in traditional medicine for treating headaches, arthritis, and stress.
  • Popular for making pandan extract, tea, and scented products.

Aloe Vera

Scientific Name: Aloe barbadensis miller

Lifespan/Growing Period:
  • Perennial, can live for 5 to 25 years. Thrives in warm, dry climates with well-drained, sandy soil. Grows slowly but steadily with minimal care.
Environmental Benefits:
  • Aloe Vera is a drought-tolerant plant that requires little water, making it ideal for water-conserving gardens. It also helps improve air quality indoors.
Health Benefits:
  • Gel from the leaves is used to treat burns, wounds, and skin irritations.
  • Known for anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and hydrating properties.
  • Popular in cosmetics, skincare, and herbal medicine.
  • Offers economic potential through products like aloe gel, juice, and creams.

Livestock-Based Products

Chicken

Scientific Name: Gallus gallus domesticus

Lifespan/Growing Period:
  • Chickens raised for meat (broilers) are typically grown for 6-8 weeks.
  • Laying hens can live up to 5-10 years, but are most productive in the first 2-3 years.
Environmental Benefits:
  • Chickens can be raised in small spaces, making them ideal for urban or backyard farming.
  • Their manure is a natural fertilizer, enriching soil with nutrients and supporting organic agriculture.
Health Benefits:
  • A source of lean protein, rich in vitamins B6, B12, and niacin.

Philippine Goat

Scientific Name: Capra aegagrus hircus

Lifespan/Growing Period:
  • Lives up to 8 to 10 years. Usually raised for meat (chevon). Can be sold at 6 months old.
Environmental Benefits:
  • Philippine native goats are resilient to local diseases, easy to manage, and well-suited to backyard or small-scale farms.
Health Benefits:
  • Source of affordable protein in rural areas.
  • Requires minimal capital to raise, perfect for smallholder farmers.
  • Contributes to food security and rural livelihood.
  • Common in community livestock dispersal programs.
  • Goat milk is rich in calcium, vitamin A, and medium-chain fatty acids.

Indian Goat

Scientific Name: Jamunapari, Boer-cross

Lifespan/Growing Period:
  • Typically 10 to 12 years. Raised for meat and milk. Reaches market weight in about 6-8 months.
Environmental Benefits:
  • Indian goat breeds are well-adapted to hot climates, require less feed, and can thrive on low-quality forage, making them suitable for sustainable farming systems.
Health Benefits:
  • Known for high meat yield and fast growth.
  • Milk is rich in nutrients, suitable for cheese and yogurt production.
  • Good for breeding programs to improve local stocks.
  • Popular in livestock livelihood programs due to their value and productivity.

Rabbit

Scientific Name: Oryctolagus cuniculus

Lifespan/Growing Period:
  • Rabbits grow quickly, reaching market size in just 10-12 weeks.
  • Lifespan is around 5-8 years.
Environmental Benefits:
  • Require minimal space and feed.
  • Their manure is high in nitrogen, making it excellent for organic compost.
Health Benefits:
  • Rabbit meat is high in protein, low in fat and cholesterol.

Tilapia

Scientific Name: Oreochromis niloticus

Lifespan/Growing Period:
  • Lifespan of 7-10 years, but market size is usually reached in 6-8 months.
Environmental Benefits:
  • Grows well in small-scale aquaponics or fishponds.
  • Tilapia helps promote sustainable water use in integrated farming systems.
Health Benefits:
  • High in protein and a good source of omega-3 fatty acids.

Pabo

Scientific Name: Meleagris gallopavo

Lifespan/Growing Period:
  • Turkeys raised for meat reach market weight in 4-6 months. Lifespan in backyard settings can be up to 10 years.
Environmental Benefits:
  • Can be raised free-range, reducing reliance on industrial feed and housing.
  • Turkey manure is nutrient-rich and can enhance soil fertility.
Health Benefits:
  • A good source of lean protein, B vitamins, and selenium.
